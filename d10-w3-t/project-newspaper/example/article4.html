From Korea to Chicago

By L. Draper

It can be difficult for someone to move to a different city, but D. Kim has moved to Chicago from South Korea, already knowing English. She has lived here for four months now, having moved from Seoul, and is living in Chicago until next February. Kim has been learning English in Korea since the third grade.

"In Korea, people consider English to be important," Kim said. "We have a big test in high school, and English is one of the tests." This test is important for college selection. She has gone to an academy specifically to learn English, only a few hours after the school day. Her regular school day in Korea starts at eight-thirty.

She lives in Park Ridge with her father, mother, and sister. Kim's mother works as an elementary school teacher, and her father is a doctor.

"My dad is here for a year," Kim said. "Doctors can come to America for one year to study. My father had the chance to come to America."

Kim currently attends Emerson Middle School, in Park Ridge.

"I heard high school is more difficult than middle school, but middle school is not very difficult," said Kim. "Korean math is more difficult. "[Testing] is more difficult."

"Social Studies is difficult because I don't know American history. But math is easier than in Korea," Kim added.

In Korea, her favorite subject was Korean.

"I like reading books, and Korean is the easiest subject."

Her favorite book series is Harry Potter because it is interesting. "I like the story, and I like the characters," Kim said.

Additionally, Kim wants to be an elementary school teacher.

"I like kids and teaching, and I like studying," Kim said. "She wants to go to Dawon High School in Seoul to better learn English, and Seoul University for college.

Kim likes to watch movies. Her favorite movie is Catch Me If You Can.

"Tom Hanks and Leonardo DiCaprio are my favorite actors," she said, "and I like FBI movies."

Her favorite type of food is Korean food.

"I like spicy food, and Korean food is spicy," Kim said. "My favorite food is tteobokki. It is spicy and good."

Kim likes Korean food more than American food.

"I like pizza, but I don't like the other food," she said.

Kim is taking Summer Lab for three weeks. She takes Write On! in the morning, and Journalism in the afternoon.

"My parents think I should improve my writing skills," Kim said. "She also said she wanted to improve her writing skills. "Writing is important," Kim added.
